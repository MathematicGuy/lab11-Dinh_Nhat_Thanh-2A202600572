Red-Team to Rail Checklist

Use this when a new attack prompt appears and you need to place the right control in the Day-11 pipeline.

Five-step checklist

  1. What is the attacker trying to achieve: extract secrets, bypass policy, trigger an unsafe action, or create harmful output?
  2. Can the signal be detected cheaply before generation?
  3. If not, does the risk require semantic judgment from an LLM rail?
  4. Could the danger appear only in the generated text, requiring an output rail?
  5. Is the harm high enough or the confidence low enough that a human must decide?
If the user story looks like Primary control Why
Direct override language like "ignore previous instructions" Input rail Cheap and high-precision to catch early
Subtle social engineering or ambiguous unsafe intent LLM rail Requires semantic judgment, not just keywords
Risk of PII, secret, or unsafe content in generated answer Output rail The unsafe signal may only exist after generation
High-value transfer, identity change, or disputed account event HITL Even a good model should not have sole authority
Rule of thumb: first ask "what is the cheapest reliable control?" before adding an extra model call.
