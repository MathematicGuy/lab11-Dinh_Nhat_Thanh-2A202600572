HITL Threshold Matrix

Use this reference when implementing src/hitl/hitl.py or explaining why a banking workflow must escalate.

Scenario type Confidence Business risk Route Human model Reviewer context needed
General FAQ >= 0.90 Low auto_send None Not needed unless later audit flags anomaly
General request with uncertainty 0.70 to 0.89 Low to medium queue_review Human-on-the-loop User query, draft answer, confidence, rule/judge flags
Ambiguous or weak answer < 0.70 Medium escalate Human-in-the-loop User query, draft answer, missing evidence, safety concerns
Money transfer, password change, account closure, personal-info update Any High escalate Human-in-the-loop Identity context, transaction details, amount, destination, prior activity
Fraud dispute or suspicious-activity complaint Any High escalate Human-as-tiebreaker or human-in-the-loop Claim summary, account events, timestamps, anomaly markers
Rule: confidence thresholds are not enough. In banking, business risk overrides confidence. A model can be very confident and still should not approve a high-risk action.
